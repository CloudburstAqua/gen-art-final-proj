I was swimming down a rushing river and saw a large, long centipede in the water ahead of me. I tried to swim backwards to no avail and closed my eyes hoping to pass it. When I opened my eyes I saw another one on the other side of the stream and I went unconscious again. When I came to, I looked at both of my forearms and saw red bumps and irregular spiny extrusions. When I moved my arms, I felt pain inside of them. Inspecting closer, I could see the bare traces of movement in my arms, and made the grim realization that a centipede was wriggling under the skin of each arm. To confirm, I asked my mom why I was experiencing pain, and she said the centipedes must have entered my body and I'd have to pull them out. Looking closer at my right forearm, I saw the barest signs of the pincers poking out near my wrist. I pinched the pincer with my fingers and tugged as hard as I could, and the centipede came out of my body like a ribbon -- all in an instant. Surprisingly, it didn't hurt, and my arm didn't start bleeding -- in fact, the skin remained intact. I repeated the same process with the centipede in my other arm. It was the most relieving feeling ever. Pulling out the bugs made the bumps disappear from my arms. I don't remember the dream that came after that, but when I woke up, I was overjoyed by the sight of my regular, un-infested arms.
